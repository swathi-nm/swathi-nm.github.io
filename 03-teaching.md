In my PhD, I have been privileged to be a [McDougal Graduate Teaching Fellow](https://poorvucenter.yale.edu/teaching/teaching-fellowships-and-funding/mcdougal-graduate-teaching-fellows) at Yale's Poorvu Center for Teaching and Learning, where I have co-facilitated various teaching workshops and learning communities on a variety of pedagogical topics for graduate students and postdoctoral fellows. I was also selected as a 2025-26 [CRC Mellon Pedagogy Fellow](https://ritm.yale.edu/programs/fellowships/crc-pedagogy-fellows) at the Yale Center for Race, Indigeneity, and Transnational Migration (RITM). 

I have also been a teaching fellow (teaching assistant) for three courses (Introduction to Ecology and Evolutionary Biology, Biology of Terrestrial Arthropods, Evolution and Medicine) and was a guest lecturer on fitness landscapes for Evolutionary Biology in Spring 2026. In Spring 2027, I will be co-teaching "Biology of Humans" with [Valerie Horsley](https://horsleylab.org/), an interdisciplinary course on the intersection of human biology, society, and medicine, as part of the [Associates in Teaching program](https://poorvucenter.yale.edu/teaching/teaching-programs-and-events/associates-in-teaching). I have also supervised an undergraduate honors thesis by Kemper Lowry, who performed a bibliometric analysis on how context-dependence has been studied in ecology and evolutionary biology.
